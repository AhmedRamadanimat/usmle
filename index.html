Histology

Study the structure of tissues and cells under the microscope

Introduction to Histology

Histology is the study of the microscopic structure of biological tissues using a microscope. It is essential for understanding normal tissue structure and identifying pathological changes in medical practice.

Main Topics

  • Basic Tissue Types

    Epithelial, connective, muscle, and nervous tissues

  • Cellular Structures

    Organelles, nucleus, cytoplasm, and cell membrane

  • Organ Systems

    Histological features of various organs

  • Microscopy Techniques

    Light microscopy, electron microscopy, and staining methods

  • Pathological Histology

    Understanding tissue changes in disease

Chemistry

Introduction to Chemistry

Chemistry is fundamental to understanding how biological molecules function and interact. From atomic structure to complex organic compounds, this course covers the chemistry essential for medical students.

Main Topics

  • Atomic Structure & Bonding

    Atoms, electrons, and chemical bonds (ionic, covalent, hydrogen)

  • Organic Chemistry

    Hydrocarbons, functional groups, and reaction mechanisms

  • Biochemistry

    Carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids

  • Chemical Reactions

    Equilibrium, kinetics, and thermodynamics

  • pH & Buffers

    Acid-base balance and biological pH regulation
